Playful Interactions with Forests: Humans and Beyond?

Humankind’s current relationship with nature could be described as being multi-faceted and at times contradictory: while we cherish nature and the rich experiences it affords, we often treat it as a mere resource to exploit, leading to an ever looming threat of ecological collapse. We are growing disconnected from nature, yet increasingly aware that we are both connected and dependent on the ecosystems that sustain us. Interestingly, tech’s role as a human-nature interaction mediator can also be ambivalent: it can enable both utilitarian, colonialist, and damaging ways of relating to nature just as much as it can support caring, joyful, and sustainable human-nature entanglements. Careful consideration of the values behind nature tech innovation seems crucial when it comes to facilitating holistically sustainable developments.
